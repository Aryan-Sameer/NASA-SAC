Habitable Zone

The Goldilocks Zone is an astronomical term for the region around a star where planets can host liquid water and possibly support life. The name comes from the idea that planets orbiting at the "just right" distance from a star are not too hot or too cold to host liquid water.
Hydrospera exists in the habitable zone.

Type of star

Hydrospera revolves around a Rubysol, G-type star. Such a star has about 0.9 to 1.1 solar masses and an effective temperature between about 5,300 and 6,000 K. Rubysol is a yellow dwarf with moderate to high luminous. It has a long sequence
Lifespan: G-type stars have a relatively long main-sequence lifetime, which means they remain stable and hydrogen-burning for billions of years. Our Sun, for example, is about 4.6 billion years old and has several billion years left in its main-sequence phase.

Transit Period

The transit period in the context of astronomy refers to the durartion of time it takesfor a celestial object to pass infront of its parent star. This event is known as transit. During transit the object partially blocks teh portion of the star's light causing a temporary dip in the star's brightness. This leads to the transit graph which is a graph between brightness and time.
This transit graph is a vital source of information for obserbation of stars through telescope, especially in exoplanets.

Molten metallic core

A molten metal core, like the one found in earth, plays a crucial role in protecting the planet from harmful radiation from te star. Earth's outer core mainly consist of molten iron and nickel, which generates a powerful magnetic field through a process known as geodynamo. this magnetic field extends into space and forms the earth's magnetosphere, a protective shield that reflects and traps charged particles from solar storm which is a stream of high energy particles created by the star.
